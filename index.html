About SAMADHAN

SAMADHAN is a National Social Innovation Challenge bringing together students from IITs, IIMs, and leading institutions to co-create solutions for NGOs and grassroots communities.

This initiative bridges the gap between academic excellence and ground-level social challenges, empowering the next generation of changemakers to develop sustainable, scalable solutions that transform lives.

Our Vision

A future where every child has a dignified childhood and equal opportunity to live, learn, and grow.

Our Mission

To develop a sustainable ecosystem for underprivileged communities by enabling, educating, and empowering children.

Focus Themes

Problem statements addressing critical social challenges

1. COMMUNITY-CENTRIC ENGAGEMENT

Many social challenges‚Äîsuch as community waste management, sanitation, education access, and public health‚Äîcannot be solved through technology or policy alone. Lasting impact happens when communities are engaged, informed, and actively involved in the process. This theme invites you to design solutions that: Spark local awareness and drive behavior change Mobilize communities for collective action Empower local leaders to lead change from within Facilitate two-way communication between NGOs and citizens From managing waste to improving public health, real transformation begins when communities become co-creators, not just recipients, of solutions. Under this theme, you‚Äôll be challenged to build solutions that make community engagement meaningful, scalable, and rooted in trust.

2.TECH-ENABLED IMPACT SOLUTIONS

NGO frontline teams work tirelessly to create change‚Äîbut their efforts are often slowed by manual processes, scattered data, and limited access to effective tech. Insights are lost, impact goes unmeasured, and valuable time is wasted. This theme challenges you to build simple, human-centered tech tools that help NGOs work smarter‚Äînot harder. Think solutions that: Make data collection seamless Turn information into action Enhance communication and outreach Function in low-tech, low-resource environments The real challenge? Designing technology that fits the realities on the ground‚Äîtools that are accessible, adaptable, and truly empowering. Rethink how tech can bridge the last mile and turn everyday work into lasting impact.

3.SKILL-DRIVEN SUSTAINABLE LIVELIHOODS

Many NGOs run skill development centers to empower women and youth through training in areas like tailoring, handicrafts, and food processing. While these centers build valuable skills, they often remain donor-dependent and lack financial sustainability. The challenge is to transform these centers into self-sustaining micro-enterprises that not only continue beyond external funding but also provide steady livelihoods to those trained. This involves moving from a training-only model to one focused on market linkages, business strategy, branding, and community ownership, enabling financial independence and long-term impact. Under this theme, you‚Äôll work on a real case study from UPAY and propose practical solutions aligned with these goals.

Timeline & Event Flow

1

Problem Understanding & Initial Solution Design

20 Aug - 07 Sep 2025

Phase 1 - Problem Understanding & Initial Solution Design

In Phase 1, participants begin by thoroughly analyzing the given problem statement. The goal is to understand the operational nuances, challenges, and constraints associated with the on-ground scenario.

Objective: Draft a solution tackling the problem statement keeping in mind practicality of the solutions.

Start: 20 Aug 25, 11:00 PM IST End: 07 Sep 25, 11:59 PM IST
2

Solution Refinement

15 Sep - 30 Sep 2025

Phase 2 - Solution Refinement

In Phase 2, the top 30 teams from across India will move forward based on the strength and potential of their initial solutions.

Selection Process: These teams were judged and top 15 teams were selected for the next phase.

Start: 15 Sep 25, 12:00 AM IST End: 30 Sep 25, 11:59 PM IST
3

Mentorship Session

10 Oct - 20 Nov 2025

Phase 3 - Mentorship Session

The top 15 teams from across India will move forward based on the strength and potential of their initial solutions. These teams will gain exclusive access to structured mentorship sessions.

Mentorship: Each team will be paired with industry leaders, social sector experts, and experienced mentors who will guide them in refining their MVPs/Ideas.

Start: 10 Oct 25, 12:00 AM IST End: 20 Nov 25, 11:59 PM IST
4

Submission and Finals

28 Dec 2025 - 4 Jan 2026

Phase 4 - Submission and Finals

Submission Deadline: 28 Dec 2025

National Finale & Solution Showcase: In the final phase, the top 15 teams will advance to the National Finals, where they will present their refined solutions to a distinguished jury.

Implementation Opportunity: Top-performing teams will be given the opportunity to implement their solutions on ground.

Finals: 4 Jan 26, 9:00 AM IST
